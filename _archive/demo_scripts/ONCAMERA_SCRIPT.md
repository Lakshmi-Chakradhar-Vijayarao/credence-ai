# On-Camera Script — LOCKED (matches 1:26 audiogram)

**Runtime**: ~55 seconds
**Delivery**: One take. Speak slower than feels natural. Pauses are intentional.

---

How many times today did you say "I think"?

How many times did you mean "I know"?

*(beat — 2 seconds)*

Hi, this is Chakradhar — independent researcher, master's graduate from UT Dallas.

I always wondered —

Uncertainty is what makes us human.

Every great discovery started with "I don't know."
Every leap forward began with the freedom to not be sure.
When we're uncertain, we wonder. We question. We imagine what could be true before we know what is.

*(pause — 0.6s)*

We say "I think" — and we keep asking.
We say "maybe" — and the world stays open.
We say "unconfirmed" — and that single word tells everyone around us: treat this differently.

*(pause — 0.6s)*

That word is an anchor.
That hedge is a Guardrail.

*(pause — 1.0s)*

Now we're building AI systems that read our uncertainty,
compress it,
summarize it,
pass it forward.
Somewhere in that pipeline — the anchor gets dropped.
The "maybe" becomes a fact.
The "I think" becomes a certainty.
The "unconfirmed" disappears.

*(pause — 0.8s)*

And unlike a human, who feels the doubt —
*(pause — 0.8s)*
the machine doesn't notice.
*(pause — 0.8s)*
It just executes.

*(beat)*

Not anymore.

There's a fix — and we call it Credence. An enforcement layer that preserves epistemic state through AI pipelines.

*(pause)*

Now I'm going to hand over to Claude — my co-researcher — to walk you through exactly how it works.

---

## Recording Notes

- **Camera**: Phone landscape, eye level, facing a window
- **Background**: Plain wall or bookshelf
- **Opening two questions**: Deliver slowly. One question. Pause. Second question. Pause. Let them sit.
- **"Not anymore."**: Full stop. Single breath. Do not rush into "There's a fix."
- **"It just executes."**: Level tone. No falling inflection. End cold.
- **"anchor" / "Guardrail"**: These replaced "hedge is load-bearing" in the audiogram version.
- **Takes**: Record 3 full takes. Do not stop mid-take. Pick the best one complete.
